## Critical Gaps & Friction Points ‚ö†Ô∏è

### 1. **Unclear Primary Use Case**
**Problem:** The landing page says "for Everyone" and lists 10+ use cases. This creates cognitive overload.

**User Mental Model:**
- "Is this a date night app?"
- "A chore manager?"
- "A random number generator?"
- "A trip planner?"

**Reality:** Users need to immediately grasp ONE compelling use case before they'll invest time learning others.

### 2. **Weak Value Proposition Hierarchy**
**Current Message:** "Stop scrolling and start doing"

**Issues:**
- Too generic (could apply to any productivity app)
- Doesn't explain WHY a jar-based system is better than notes/lists
- Doesn't highlight the unique AI concierge value

### 3. **Missing Emotional Hook**
**Current Approach:** Feature-focused ("Here's what it does")

**Missing:** Story-driven ("Here's the problem you face")

**Example:**
- ‚ùå "Organize ideas and spin to decide"
- ‚úÖ "Tired of spending 30 minutes choosing where to eat while your food gets cold? Let us decide for you."

### 4. **Low Discovery & Virality**
**Current Gaps:**
- No social sharing features (e.g., "Share this restaurant recommendation")
- No public jars or templates (e.g., "Top 50 Date Ideas in NYC - Copy to Your Jar")
- No referral incentives
- No content marketing (blog, Instagram, TikTok examples)

### 5. **Friction in Onboarding**
**Current Flow:** Sign Up ‚Üí Empty Dashboard ‚Üí Add Ideas manually

**Problem:** Users see an empty screen and don't know where to start.

**Better Flow:** Sign Up ‚Üí "Pick a template" ‚Üí Pre-populated jar ‚Üí Immediate value
